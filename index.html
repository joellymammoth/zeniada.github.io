About

Zeniada Literary Magazine

Founded in 1929, Zeniada is Johns Hopkins University's oldest and only intercollegiate literary magazine. Every spring and fall, we publish original poetry, prose, and artwork from undergraduates across the country.

Zeniada is seeking students for the following positions. Applicants must be currently pursuing an undergraduate degree at Johns Hopkins University.


Editor (Spring 2017)

Editors review poetry, prose, and/or artwork for each issue of Zeniada. They are required to attend weekly meetings (1/1.5 hours) to discuss submissions and may have to read said submissions on their own time. Editors who have at least one year of experience with Zeniada recieve the title of "senior editors."

Submit

Zeniada accepts poetry, prose, and anything in between (such as prose poetry or flash fiction) as well as artwork. All submissions are judged anonymously. Anyone who is enrolled in an undergraduate program for the current school year is welcome to submit.

Zeniada is currently seeking submissions for Spring 2017. Please email prose, poetry, and artwork to submit@zeniada.com.


Submission Guidelines


1. The subject of your email should be the category of your work.

So Poetry, Prose, or Art. Prose poetry should be submitted under Poetry, and flash fiction should be submitted under Prose.

2. In the body of the email, please include:

- Your name
- Your expected year of graduation
- The name of the undergraduate school you currently attend
- If you're submitting artwork, the medium/media used
Any special instructions or comments regarding your work should appear in the same document as your work.

3. If you are submitting writing, attach your work as a Word document.

Your name, school, or any other type of identification should not appear anywhere in this document.
